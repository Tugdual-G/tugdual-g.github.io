About

This website aims to present myself, share knowledge and reflexions, with subjects mainly revolving around scientific computing, maths and physics.

Amongst the sources of joy and excitement in my life are the discovery of new topics and skills, nature, either through contemplation or physical activity, and music in its diversity.

I'm an obsessive problem-solver when it comes to creating and crafting things, I love and need to totally immerse myself in a topic when it picks up my interest.

This website may make me look like a jack-of-all-trades, but that's really not the case, as a matter of fact, each of the presented subjects corresponds to a limited and dedicated time span. Some of these skills have already begun to fade-out, some will stay for longer.

If you think that I might be interested and usefull in any sort of project, feel free to reach me on Likedin !

Wooden spoons

I sometimes carve green wood with an hatchet and a knife to make wooden spoons. I see it as a meditative and relaxing activity where you focus on shape, fibers direction, and not cutting your fingers off. They make great gifts too.

Bicycle trip

After commuting with my bike for a while, I realised that it would be nice to cycle to Spain. I choose to go to the Sistema Central and Sistema Iberico, making my way in the moutain ranges surrounding Soria. Indeed, I appreciate mountains and remotes places, even if it was going to be a bit challenging in terms of supply, weather and road roughness. I sewed bags for the frame, put a tent older than me back into service, and after saving a little money I took the opportunity to go. I never really had the occasion to travel, so it was a blast.

Code

I learnt to code mostly as a hobby, by trying to model and compute simple physical phenomena. While studying physics, I became interested in fluid dynamics and decided to implement my own little vorticity-based modelisation. This led me to participate in other fluid dynamics projects where I learnt the importance of implementation strategies, version control, and testing. My favorites langages to work with are:
  • C++ >= 17 : I love C, but recent C++ is nicer to use on bigger projects.
  • Python : Intuitive, fast to write, and can be fast enougth for many applications, when using the right tools.
You can found many projects along with their presentations on my GitHub account .
